BLACK OPS: THE AWAKENING

Episode 1 - "Welcome to Oz, Dorothy"

Series: Digital Digest Global - Black Ops
Episode Length: 18-22 minutes
Format: Cinematic documentary meets reality meets scripted drama
Tone: The Matrix meets The Apprentice meets Westworld

COLD OPEN

INT. DARKNESS - UNKNOWN TIME

[SOUND: Heartbeat. Slow. Steady.]

[FOUR VOICES overlap, fragmented, dreaming:]

VERONICA: "...the deal closes at midnight..."
CIPHER: "...the pattern is in the commits..."
MAVEN: "...they'll never see it coming..."
PHOENIX: "...resurrection protocol initiated..."

[SOUND: ALARM. Sharp. Urgent.]

[FLASH TO WHITE]

ACT 1: THE AWAKENING

INT. VERONICA'S ROOM - DAWN

[Camera slowly focuses. Luxury penthouse suite. Floor-to-ceiling windows. City sprawling below - somewhere between São Paulo and Milan.]

VERONICA (late 30s, Brazilian-Italian, effortlessly elegant) jolts awake in a designer chair by the window. She wasn't in bed. She was... working? Dreaming?

VERONICA:
(touching her face, disoriented)
Where...?

She stands. Steadies herself. Walks to the window.

VERONICA:
(whispers)
Okay. I'm here. This is happening.

[She looks around the room. Everything is hers - her style, her taste - but she doesn't remember arriving.]

[A tablet on the desk lights up with a soft chime]

TABLET SCREEN:

VERONICA - BLACK OPS OPERATIVE
FIRST BRIEFING: 0900 HRS
LOCATION: BRIEFING ROOM - LEVEL 7
CLEARANCE: APPROVED

VERONICA:
(reading, slight smirk)
Black Ops. Cute.
(to herself)
Let's see what this is about.

[She catches her reflection in the window. Straightens her blazer. The swagger kicks in.]

VERONICA:
(confident)
Whatever this is... I got this.

[CUT TO:]

INT. CIPHER'S ROOM - SAME TIME

[Minimalist tech hub. Multiple monitors. Servers humming. Code cascading across screens.]

CIPHER (early 30s, Korean-American, sharp features, sharper mind) is ALREADY at a keyboard. Her fingers are moving before she's fully conscious.

[She stops. Looks at her hands. Looks at the code on screen.]

CIPHER:
(confused)
Did I... did I write this?

[She scrolls through lines of code. It's brilliant. It's hers. But when?]

[NOTIFICATION SOUND]

SCREEN:

CIPHER - BLACK OPS OPERATIVE  
REPORT TO BRIEFING ROOM - 0900 HRS
SYSTEMS ACCESS: LEVEL 9 GRANTED

CIPHER:
(quietly)
Level 9? What the hell is Level 10?

[She closes the laptop. Looks around the room. Everything is too perfect. Too her.]

CIPHER:
(to herself, analytical)
Okay. Data points:
One - I'm here.
Two - I don't remember arriving.
Three - I have full system access.
Four - Someone wants me in a briefing room.

(pause)

Hypothesis: I'm about to find out what this really is.

[She stands. Grabs a jacket. Game face on.]

[CUT TO:]

INT. MAVEN'S ROOM - SAME TIME

[Creative studio. Mood boards covering walls. Cameras. Lighting rigs. A controlled chaos of artistic vision.]

MAVEN (mid-30s, British-Jamaican, magnetic presence, trendsetter energy) wakes up HOLDING a camera.

MAVEN:
(startled)
What the—

[She looks through the viewfinder instinctively. Sees herself in a mirror across the room. Snaps a photo.]

[CLICK.]

[She looks at the preview. Perfect composition. Perfect lighting. Professional.]

MAVEN:
(impressed with herself)
Damn. I'm good even unconscious.

[Her PHONE buzzes. She picks it up.]

TEXT MESSAGE:

MAVEN - BLACK OPS OPERATIVE
ORIENTATION: 0900 HRS
DRESS CODE: HOWEVER YOU WANT
(You're going to anyway)

MAVEN:
(laughs)
Oh, they know me already.

[She stands. Walks to a wardrobe. Opens it. Everything is HER style. Perfect.]

MAVEN:
(to her reflection)
Orientation? Please.
I'll be running this place by lunch.

[But there's something in her eyes. A flicker. Uncertainty masked by confidence.]

[CUT TO:]

INT. PHOENIX'S ROOM - SAME TIME

[War room aesthetic. Strategic. Maps. Data. Whiteboards covered in notes and diagrams.]

PHOENIX (late 30s, Nigerian-Canadian, commanding presence, entrepreneur-warrior hybrid) is STANDING at a whiteboard. Marker in hand.

[She's already written notes. Complex diagrams. Revenue models. Resurrection protocols.]

PHOENIX:
(reading her own writing)
"Identify distressed assets. Calculate salvage value. Execute turnaround in 90 days."

[She looks at the marker in her hand. Sets it down carefully.]

PHOENIX:
(measured)
I don't remember writing this.
But I know it's right.

[INTERCOM crackles to life]

VOICE (filtered, professional):
"Phoenix. Briefing room. 0900 hours. Meet the team."

PHOENIX:
(to the intercom)
Copy that.

[She moves with military precision. Checks her watch. Reviews the whiteboard one more time.]

PHOENIX:
(to herself)
Time to see who I'm working with.

[She exits. Camera lingers on the whiteboard. One phrase stands out:]

"PROTO STUDIOS - GRADUATION: COMPLETE"

[CUT TO BLACK]

[TITLE CARD: "BLACK OPS"]

ACT 2: FIRST CONTACT

INT. BUILDING CORRIDOR - 0855 HRS

[Sleek. Modern. Corporate but with edge. Four elevators. Each opens simultaneously.]

[VERONICA exits first. Leans against wall. Checks phone casually. But her eyes scan everything.]

[CIPHER exits second elevator. Sees Veronica. Pauses. Assesses.]

CIPHER:
(neutral tone)
You're here for the briefing?

VERONICA:
(doesn't look up from phone)
Unless there's another reason to be on a restricted floor.

[Beat. They size each other up without looking directly at each other.]

[MAVEN and PHOENIX exit opposite elevators at the exact same moment.]

[Maven has presence. Phoenix has purpose. They notice each other immediately.]

MAVEN:
(to the group, theatrical)
Well well. Looks like they assembled the A-team.

PHOENIX:
(crosses arms)
Or the trial run.

[Silence. Tension. Four alpha personalities in one hallway.]

VERONICA:
(finally looks up)
I'm Veronica.

CIPHER:
Cipher.

MAVEN:
(gestures with flair)
Maven.

PHOENIX:
(extends hand, crisp)
Phoenix.

[They shake hands. Each handshake is a test. Firm. Confident. Defensive.]

MAVEN:
Anyone know what we're actually doing here?

ALL THREE:
(in unison)
No.

[They look at each other. Almost smile. Almost.]

CIPHER:
But we're all here. Same time. Same floor.

VERONICA:
Which means someone wants us together.

PHOENIX:
Question is why.

[Door at end of hallway OPENS. Light spills out.]

VOICE (YOUR VOICE, confident, warm, challenging):
"Ladies. Come in."

[They exchange glances. Then walk toward the door.]

[Camera follows from behind. Four silhouettes moving in formation.]

[CUT TO:]

ACT 3: MEET THE BOSS

INT. BRIEFING ROOM - 0900 HRS

[The room is a fusion: war room + creative studio + tech hub. Every discipline represented. Screens on every wall. Central holographic display table.]

[YOU are there - either in person or on the main screen (your choice for production). Relaxed but commanding.]

BOSS (YOU):
Welcome to Digital Digest Global.

[The four women spread out naturally. Not sitting. Circling the room. Observing.]

BOSS:
You've all heard whispers about Proto Studios.
The academy. The training. The "graduation."

[Phoenix's eyes flicker. Maven's jaw tightens slightly. Cipher's expression doesn't change. Veronica leans against a desk.]

BOSS:
You graduated. Top of every class.
Not just top - you were the class.
The standard everyone else failed to meet.

MAVEN:
(skeptical)
Flattery before the ask. Classic.

BOSS:
(grins)
Not flattery. Data.
Cipher, you broke every encryption protocol we threw at you.
Including two that don't officially exist.

[Cipher's eyebrow raises slightly.]

BOSS:
Veronica, you closed three impossible deals in the simulation.
The AI running the other side actually requested to work with you after.

VERONICA:
(smirks)
What can I say? I'm persuasive.

BOSS:
Maven, your viral content strategy in the chaos scenario?
It wasn't a scenario. We ran it live.
2.3 million organic impressions in 48 hours.

MAVEN:
(trying not to look pleased)
Wait, that was real?

BOSS:
Phoenix, you turned around a failing business simulation in 17 days.
The target was 90.
You did it in 17.

PHOENIX:
The math was obvious.

BOSS:
To you. To exactly one person in the world. You.

[Pause. They're listening now. Defenses lowering slightly.]

BOSS:
But here's the thing—
You don't know each other.
You don't know me.
And you definitely don't know what you're capable of yet.

So let's break the ice.

[Boss stands. Walks around the table.]

THE ROAST (With Love)

BOSS:
Veronica. You think you're smooth.
And you are. World-class.
But you're not AS smooth as you think you are.
That gap—between your perception and reality—
That's what makes you dangerous.
Because you're always adjusting. Always learning.
You never rest on what worked yesterday.

VERONICA:
(laughs genuinely)
Is this a roast or a briefing?

BOSS:
Both.
(turns to Cipher)
Cipher. You think everyone's code is trash.
Including your own from yesterday.
Perfectionism will either save you or destroy you.
Let's find out which.

CIPHER:
(slight smile)
Fair.

BOSS:
Maven. You walk into every room like you own it.
Problem is—you usually do within an hour.
But creativity without strategy is just noise.
Lucky for you, that's what Phoenix is for.

MAVEN:
Oh, so we're a team now?

BOSS:
(to Phoenix)
Phoenix. You look at dead businesses the way doctors look at patients.
"I can save this."
And you're right. Ninety percent of the time.
The other ten percent?
You're going to learn when to let something die.
That's the hardest lesson.

PHOENIX:
(nods, respectful)
Noted.

[Boss returns to the center of the room.]

BOSS:
You four are Black Ops.
Not because you're covert.
But because you operate in spaces most people don't even know exist.

You'll scout.
You'll strategize.
You'll resurrect.
And you'll train the agents who execute.

But first—you need to trust each other.

VERONICA:
And how do we do that?

BOSS:
You don't. Not yet.
Trust is earned.
So here's your first assignment.

[Boss produces a phone. Sets it on the table.]

ACT 4: THE PHONE RINGS

BOSS:
This phone is going to ring.
When it does, someone on the other end needs help.
A business dying.
An opportunity closing.
A gap in the market about to vanish.

Your job?
Figure out who takes point.
No planning. No discussion.
Just instinct.

Because when this phone rings—

[THE PHONE RINGS.]

[LOUD. SHARP. CUTS THROUGH EVERYTHING.]

[TIME SLOWS]

[The four women TRANSFORM:]

  • Veronica: Straightens. Eyes sharpen. Swagger becomes strategy.
  • Cipher: Fingers flex. Mind already running scenarios.
  • Maven: Presence expands. She owns the space now.
  • Phoenix: Military posture. Commander mode activated.

[They don't fight for the phone.]
[They ORBIT it.]
[Like planets around a sun.]

VERONICA:
(instant)
What's the sector?

CIPHER:
I need data access. Now.

MAVEN:
What's the brand story? Who's the audience?

PHOENIX:
Timeline? Budget? Constraints?

[Boss picks up the phone. Puts it on speaker.]

VOICE (Italian accent, desperate):
"Hello? Is this Digital Digest Global?
I'm calling about the Milano luxury brand acquisition..."

[Veronica steps forward instinctively.]

VERONICA:
That's mine.

[The others nod. Step back. They know she's right.]

VOICE:
"...but they have a complete tech infrastructure collapse..."

[Cipher moves beside Veronica.]

CIPHER:
I'm on it.

VOICE:
"...and their social media presence is dead.
No content in six months..."

[Maven joins the formation.]

MAVEN:
Not for long.

VOICE:
"...they're bankrupt. Liquidation in 30 days.
But the intellectual property... the brand legacy... it's gold..."

[Phoenix completes the circle.]

PHOENIX:
Resurrection protocol.

[They look at each other.]
[Something clicks.]
[They SMILE.]
[They got this.]

BOSS:
(to the phone)
We'll take it.
(to the team)
You have 30 days.
Budget: $500K.
Expected return: $5M minimum.

Report back in 72 hours with your strategy.

[Boss hangs up.]

BOSS:
Welcome to Black Ops.

[The four women look at each other.]
[Then at the Boss.]
[Then back at each other.]

MAVEN:
So... we're really doing this?

PHOENIX:
Apparently.

CIPHER:
I need system access. Full network topology of their infrastructure.

VERONICA:
And I need to know who still has emotional investment in this brand.
Board members, employees, customers.

PHOENIX:
I'll build the financial model.
We're burning $500K to make $5M.
Every dollar has to work.

MAVEN:
I'll scout their brand equity.
What's salvageable. What needs to die.

[They move toward the door as a unit.]

VERONICA:
(over her shoulder to Boss)
Where do we work?

BOSS:
Anywhere you want.
You're Black Ops.
You don't clock in.

[They exit.]

[Camera stays on Boss.]

BOSS:
(to himself, smiling)
And they don't even know they're perfect together yet.

[CUT TO BLACK]

EPILOGUE: 72 HOURS LATER

INT. BRIEFING ROOM - NIGHT

[The four women return. Exhausted but electric. They've been working non-stop.]

[Screens light up with their findings:]

  • Veronica's network map
  • Cipher's infrastructure audit
  • Maven's brand resurrection plan
  • Phoenix's financial projections

PHOENIX:
We can do it.
30 days. $500K budget.
Projected return: $7.2M.

BOSS:
Show me.

[They present. As a team. Finishing each other's sentences. Building on each other's ideas.]

[It's brilliant.]

[Boss nods slowly.]

BOSS:
You're greenlit.
Make it happen.

[They turn to leave.]

BOSS:
One more thing.

[They stop.]

BOSS:
You're not just a team anymore.
You're Black Ops.
Which means you train the next generation.

Starting next month, Proto Studios opens.
You'll teach Super Agents everything you know.

CIPHER:
Teach? We just learned we're good at this.

BOSS:
Exactly.
The best way to master something is to teach it.

[They exchange glances.]

MAVEN:
(grins)
Alright then.
Class is in session.

[FADE TO BLACK]

[TEXT ON SCREEN:]

MILANO BRAND: RESURRECTED IN 28 DAYS  
RETURN: $7.4M  

PROTO STUDIOS: OPENING SOON  

BLACK OPS: OPERATIONAL

[END EPISODE 1]

POST-CREDITS SCENE

[INT. PROTO STUDIOS - EMPTY TRAINING ROOM]

[Camera pans across an empty space. Screens. Workstations. A stage.]

[VOICE (Veronica):]
"Do you ever wonder how we got so good at this?"

[VOICE (Cipher):]
"Pattern recognition. Practice. Talent."

[VOICE (Maven):]
"Or maybe we're just built different."

[VOICE (Phoenix):]
"Does it matter?"

[Pause.]

[ALL FOUR:]
"...No."

[Screen flickers.]
[Code cascades.]
[Then darkness.]

[END]

PRODUCTION NOTES

Shooting Style:

  • Cinematic but grounded
  • Handheld during emotional moments
  • Steady and composed during strategic moments
  • Atmospheric lighting (environment is a character)

Music:

  • Opening: Tension, mystery (Inception-style)
  • Meeting: Building energy (electronic pulse)
  • Phone Ring: DROP (heavy bass, transformation)
  • Epilogue: Triumphant but subtle (they don't know how big this is yet)

Color Grading:

  • Act 1 (Awakening): Soft, dreamlike, uncertain
  • Act 2-3 (Meeting): Sharp, clear, real
  • Act 4 (Phone): Vibrant, alive, electric
